Identity

John Maus was born on 23 February 1980 in Austin, Minnesota. He is an American musician, singer, songwriter, composer and academic.

Genres

His music is associated with synth-pop, hypnagogic pop, post-punk, experimental pop and lo-fi. These genres are encoded as concepts because they describe both musical style and cultural classification.

Education

John Maus studied at California Institute of the Arts, European Graduate School and University of Hawaiʻi. At California Institute of the Arts, he met Ariel Pink.

Related People

The project records Ariel Pink as a collaborator, Panda Bear as a performance connection, and Alain Badiou as a philosophical reference.

Organizations

John Maus is connected to Ariel Pink's Haunted Graffiti, Ribbon Music, Upset the Rhythm and Young.

Albums

Important albums include Songs, Love Is Real, We Must Become the Pitiless Censors of Ourselves, A Collection of Rarities and Previously Unreleased Material, Screen Memories, Addendum and Later Than You Think.

Textual Phenomenon

The album title We Must Become the Pitiless Censors of Ourselves is encoded as a quotation and citation because it refers to Fifteen Theses on Contemporary Art by Alain Badiou.
